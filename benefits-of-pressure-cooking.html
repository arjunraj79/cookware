The Benefits of Pressure Cooking

What is Pressure Cooking?

Regular open-pot cooking is carried out at sea level at 100°C (212°F), which is the boiling temperature of water, which generates steam. By confining the steam inside a pot, pressure cooking raises the boiling point of water and, consequently, the cooking temperature by causing a safe, controlled rise in pressure. Through permeating the food, the steam preserves nutrients, color, texture, and fluids while also tenderizing and flavoring it while speeding up the cooking process. Using a pressure of 15 pounds per square inch (psi), or 1 kilogram per square centimeter, the ONS Pressure Cooker cooks food at 121 degrees Celsius (250 degrees Fahrenheit).

The Benefits of ONS Pressure Cooking

Compared to standard cooking periods, ONS pressure cooking can cut them in half. Low-cost goods like tough pieces of meat and legumes (lentils, dried peas, and beans) may be cooked to perfection in a fraction of the time it would normally take. With a pressure cooker, you save money by using less fuel because food cooks more quickly. There is scientific evidence that pressure cooking preserves several nutrients, including proteins and vitamins. Low-calorie, low-fat cooking is best done via steaming. Food prepared under pressure is more sanitary due to the greater temperature. Cooking food in a closed environment with extremely hot steam may enhance its inherent flavors and yield delectable outcomes. You may use your ONS to prepare a variety of dishes, whether they are whole meals or components of recipes.

Advantages of ONS

Safe

  • Pressure-locked Safety Lid: Similar to a contemporary airplane door, the cooker's lid is pressure-locked when there is pressure within. Before the pressure drops to a safe level, it cannot be opened.
  • Automatic Safety Valve: When it operates, food and steam are safely directed downward.

Better Pressure Regulation

  • New Improved Pressure Regulator/Vent Weight: Better controls pressure, cooks faster, reduces fuel usage, is easier to install/remove, and decreases dal foaming. Available in all ONS variants except Stainless Steel and Big PEXA.

Trouble-Free

  • Longer Lasting Sealing Ring/Gasket: The inside-fitting lid prevents sideways rubbing of the sealing ring and protects it from food acids.
  • Rust Proof Components: Handle brackets and special metal alloy handle bar prevent corrosion.

Important Safeguards

  1. Prior to usage, read all directions.
  2. Refrain from touching hot surfaces. Use handles.
  3. Close supervision is required when used near children.
  4. Do not place in a hot oven.
  5. Avoid using a dishwasher.
  6. Use extreme caution when moving hot liquids.
  7. Use only as directed.
  8. Ensure cooker is properly closed before use.
  9. Ensure safety valve, vent tube, and weight are clear.
  10. Never place anything on the vent weight.
  11. Attach vent weight only after consistent steam appears.
  12. Always add the required amount of water before pressure cooking.
  13. Do not overfill beyond two-thirds capacity.
  14. Avoid cooking foaming foods like oats or pasta.
  15. Lower heat after full pressure is achieved.
  16. Never force the cooker to open.
  17. Never lift vent weight for frothy foods.
  18. Avoid pressure frying in oil.
  19. Do not use cooker for baking or dry heating over 20 minutes.
  20. Ensure handles are secure.
  21. Use only authentic ONS safety valves.
  22. Only authorized service centers should perform repairs.

Easy Tips for Better Cooking

Cut food evenly, combine similar cooking-time foods, reduce liquid usage, and season lightly as pressure cooking enhances flavor.

Care & Cleaning

Wash manually with mild soap. Avoid dishwasher use. Dry thoroughly after cleaning. Clean steam vent regularly.

Product Certification

ONS pressure cookers are certified by Underwriters Laboratories (UL) and the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS).

Do-it-Yourself Repairs

Only gasket, safety valve, vent weight, and handles may be replaced by users using genuine ONS parts.
