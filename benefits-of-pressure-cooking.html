The Benefits of Pressure Cooking

What is Pressure Cooking?

Regular open-pot cooking is carried out at sea level at 100°C (212°F), which is the boiling temperature of water, which generates steam. By confining the steam inside a pot, pressure cooking raises the boiling point of water and, consequently, the cooking temperature by causing a safe, controlled rise in pressure. Through permeating the food, the steam preserves nutrients, color, texture, and fluids while also tenderizing and flavoring it while speeding up the cooking process. Using a pressure of 15 pounds per square inch (psi), or 1 kilogram per square centimeter, the ONS Pressure Cooker cooks food at 121 degrees Celsius (250 degrees Fahrenheit).

The Benefits of ONS Pressure Cooking

Compared to standard cooking periods, ONS pressure cooking can cut them in half. Low-cost goods like tough pieces of meat and legumes (lentils, dried peas, and beans) may be cooked to perfection in a fraction of the time it would normally take. With a pressure cooker, you save money by using less fuel because food cooks more quickly. There is scientific evidence that pressure cooking preserves several nutrients, including proteins and vitamins. Low-calorie, low-fat cooking is best done via steaming. Food prepared under pressure is more sanitary due to the greater temperature. Cooking food in a closed environment with extremely hot steam may enhance its inherent flavors and yield delectable outcomes. You may use your ONS to prepare a variety of dishes, whether they are whole meals or components of recipes.

Advantages of ONS

Safe

  • Pressure-locked Safety Lid. Similar to a contemporary airplane door, the cooker's lid is pressure-locked when there is pressure within. Before the pressure drops to a safe level, it cannot be opened.
  • Automatic Safety Valve is placed so that, when it works, the food and steam are securely sent downward.

Better Pressure Regulation

  • New Improved Pressure Regulator/Vent Weight better controls pressure, cooks more quickly, uses less fuel, is simpler to install and uninstall, and decreases the growth of dal. Except for Stainless Steel and Big PEXA, this is available in all ONS variants.

Trouble-Free

  • Longer Lasting Sealing Ring/Gasket. Because the lid fits from the inside, opening and closing the pressure cooker does not rub the sealing ring sideways. Additionally, the lid's rim protects the sealing ring from the food acids in the pressure cooker.
  • Rust Proof Components. To prevent corrosion, the ONS Pressure Cooker has handle brackets and a unique metal alloy handle bar.

Important Safeguards

  1. Prior to usage, read all directions.
  2. Refrain from touching hot surfaces. Put handles to use.
  3. The use of the pressure cooker in close proximity to youngsters requires close monitoring.
  4. The pressure cooker should not be placed in a hot oven.
  5. Avoid using a dishwasher for laundry.
  6. Shifting a pressure cooker that contains hot liquids requires the utmost caution.
  7. Only use the pressure cooker as directed.
  8. Pressure is used to cook in this gadget. Scalding injuries can develop from improper usage. Before you start using the device, make sure it is properly closed. Learn "How to Close and Open the ONS" underneath "Operating Instructions".
  9. Prior to shutting the lid for pressure cooking, make sure the safety valve, vent tube, and vent weight are clear (not clogged) at all times.
  10. While cooking, never put anything on top of the vent weight.
  11. Do not place the vent weight or other pressure control device on the vent tube when you begin pressure cooking. Only once the steam begins to flow consistently out of the vent tube should you place the vent weight on it. Observe "Trial Run" in "Operating Instructions".
  12. Prior to pressure cooking, always fill the pressure cooker body with water as indicated by the instructions...
  13. The unit shouldn't be more than two thirds filled...
  14. Note that the pressure release mechanism (vent tube) may get clogged by foods that foam, froth, and splutter...
  15. To prevent the entire liquid that produces the steam from evaporating, reduce the heat after the standard operating pressure is attained.
  16. The pressure cooker should not be opened by force...
  17. When dealing with liquid or frothy meals, never raise the vent weight to lower the pressure.
  18. Avoid using a pressure cooker for oil pressure cooking.
  19. Because the metal may weaken to a dangerous degree, never use the cooker body for deep frying, light frying, or as an oven for dry heating or baking for longer than 20 minutes at a time.
  20. Verify that the handles are secure...
  21. It is necessary to employ an authentic ONS safety valve in place of a fused safety valve...
  22. An authorized service center or agent is required to do all repairs except replacing the gasket, body handle, safety valve, and vent weight...
