Do-it-Yourself Repairs

Only an Authorized Service Center or representative is permitted to do repairs other than replacing the gasket, body handles, safety valve, and vent weight. Genuine ONS components are required for every replacement.

The notes below are helpful if you need to change certain ONS pressure cooker parts yourself.

Gasket Replacement

Replace the gasket if leaking occurs along the cooker rim and is NOT due to lid misalignment, dents, or lid curl. Always use an authentic ONS gasket that matches your cooker size.

Safety Valve Replacement

Use only a genuine ONS safety valve.

One or two 19 mm (3/4 inch) spanners may be required—one for the nut on top of the lid, and one for the retainer underneath.

TO REMOVE

1. Remove the vent weight and gasket from the lid.

2. Support the lid on a soft surface such as a folded towel. Avoid bending the lid curl or gripping the lid by its handle.

3. Place the spanner around the nut under the handle bar and press counterclockwise.

4. Hold the retainer below with your thumb while loosening the nut on top (Images 2 & 3).

5. If still tight, use a second spanner on the retainer (Images 4 & 5). Remove nut, retainer, and washer — do NOT reuse old parts.

TO REPLACE

1. Clean and dry the safety valve hole area.

2. Remove the new replacement nut from the silicone-washer retainer.

3. Insert the retainer (with silicone washer) from below the lid and tighten the nut clockwise by hand.

4. Hold the lid firmly and tighten the nut using a spanner (Image 7). Do NOT overtighten.

5. If still loose: flip the lid and use two spanners — one on the retainer, one on the nut (Images 8 & 9).

Replacement of Plastic Handles

Both the main body handle and the auxiliary handle can be replaced by unscrewing the old handles and screwing on the new ones.

For the main body handle, position the two plastic halves around the metal bracket and tighten the screws.

If removal is difficult, one person should hold the cooker body firmly while another loosens the screws.
